## What is the quick answer?

**Quick Answer:** Make pressed flower bookmarks by pressing small flat flowers for one to three weeks, arranging them on heavy paper, fixing them with tiny dots of acid-free glue, and sealing with laminate, clear adhesive film, or a transparent sleeve. Use violas, alyssum, forget-me-nots, ferns, and thin petals.

## Which flowers press best for bookmarks?

Small, flat flowers work best. Violas, pansies, alyssum, forget-me-nots, cosmos petals, larkspur petals, and small fern fronds dry neatly. Thick flowers such as roses and carnations must be separated into petals before pressing.

Avoid juicy stems and bulky flower centres. They dry slowly and can stain paper.

## How do you press flowers for a bookmark?

Place flowers between absorbent paper inside a heavy book or flower press. Keep petals flat and separate. Change damp paper after the first day if the flowers are thick or fresh.

Most small flowers are ready in one to three weeks. They should feel dry and papery before you use them.

## How should you seal a pressed flower bookmark?

Use heavy cardstock as the base. Place flowers with tweezers, use minimal glue, and let everything dry. Then seal with a laminator, clear adhesive film, or a transparent bookmark sleeve.

If you prefer a matte handmade look, leave the bookmark unlaminated and use it gently. Unsealed pressed flowers are more fragile.

## What questions do crafters ask about pressed bookmarks?

### Can fresh flowers go straight into a bookmark?

No. Fresh flowers must be fully pressed and dried first or they will stain and decay.

### What paper is best for bookmarks?

Heavy cardstock or watercolour paper works well because it stays flat and resists wrinkling.

### Can you laminate pressed flowers?

Yes, if the flowers are fully dry and not too bulky.

### How long do pressed flower bookmarks last?

Sealed bookmarks can last for years if kept away from strong sun and moisture.
