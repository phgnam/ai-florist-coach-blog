## What is the quick answer?

**Quick Answer:** Make a small pollinator planter with a deep container, pesticide-free potting mix, and compact flowers that bloom in sequence. Use alyssum, calendula, dwarf zinnias, basil flowers, thyme, compact salvia, nasturtium, and dwarf sunflowers. Choose single or semi-single flowers so bees can reach pollen and nectar.

## Which flowers fit a small pollinator planter?

Use compact plants with accessible flowers. Sweet alyssum feeds small beneficial insects. Calendula and dwarf zinnias give open blooms. Compact salvia attracts bees. Nasturtium trails over the edge and offers edible flowers. Basil and thyme can flower if you let some stems bloom.

Avoid highly doubled flowers where petals hide the centre. They may look full but offer less food.

## How large should the container be?

Use a trough or pot at least 25-30 cm deep. Small shallow bowls dry too quickly on balconies. A larger soil volume keeps roots cooler and flowers blooming longer.

Make sure the container drains freely. Pollinator flowers still need oxygen around their roots.

## How do you keep pollinators visiting all season?

Plant for succession. Include early alyssum or calendula, summer zinnias and herbs, and late salvia if your climate allows. Deadhead some flowers for more bloom, but let a few herbs and annuals keep flowering for insects.

Do not use broad insecticides on the planter. Even organic sprays can harm visiting insects when applied carelessly.

## What questions do balcony gardeners ask about pollinator planters?

### Will a small balcony planter really help bees?

Yes. It is not a habitat by itself, but it adds food in an urban area.

### Are bees dangerous on a balcony?

Most foraging bees ignore people. Avoid blocking their flight path and do not swat at them.

### Should you include herbs?

Yes. Flowering basil, thyme, oregano, and chives are excellent for small pollinators.

### Can this planter grow in partial sun?

Yes, but choose alyssum, calendula, violas, and herbs that tolerate the available light.
