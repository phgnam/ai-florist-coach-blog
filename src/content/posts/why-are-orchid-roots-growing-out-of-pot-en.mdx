## Quick Answer

Orchid roots growing out of the pot are usually normal aerial roots, especially on Phalaenopsis orchids. They absorb moisture and help anchor the plant. Do not cut firm green, silver, or white roots. Repot only if the potting bark has broken down, roots are rotting, or the plant is unstable.

## Are roots outside an orchid pot a problem?

Most of the time, no. Many common orchids are epiphytes in nature, meaning they grow attached to trees rather than buried in soil. Their roots are adapted to air, rain, and bark surfaces. A root that grows out of the pot is often doing exactly what orchid roots are built to do.

Healthy aerial roots feel firm. They may look silvery when dry and turn green after watering. Soft, hollow, black, or mushy roots are the ones that signal trouble.

## Should you cut orchid aerial roots?

Do not cut healthy aerial roots for appearance. Removing them reduces the plant's ability to take up water and can introduce infection through fresh wounds.

Cut only dead roots during repotting. Use clean scissors or pruners, and remove roots that are mushy, papery, hollow, or foul-smelling. Leave firm roots even if they look untidy.

## When should you repot an orchid with roots outside the pot?

Repot when the bark mix has decomposed into fine, damp particles; when water no longer drains freely; or when many roots inside the pot are dead. Repotting just because roots are visible is unnecessary.

Choose a pot only one size larger. Orchids prefer air around the roots, and a large pot filled with wet bark can stay damp too long. Fresh orchid bark is usually better than ordinary potting soil.

## How should you water exposed orchid roots?

Water the potting mix thoroughly, then let it drain. Aerial roots can be misted or briefly rinsed, but misting alone is not enough for the whole plant. If roots turn green after watering, they are absorbing moisture.

Let the roots dry slightly between waterings. Constantly wet roots are more dangerous than exposed roots.

## What questions do orchid owners ask about aerial roots?

### Are orchid aerial roots a sign the pot is too small?

Not always. Aerial roots are normal even when the pot size is correct.

### Can you tuck aerial roots back into the pot?

Only if they bend easily. Forcing stiff roots can crack them.

### Why are orchid roots shrivelling outside the pot?

They may be drying too much in very dry air, or they may have been damaged earlier and are no longer functioning.

### What colour should healthy orchid roots be?

Healthy roots are usually silvery-white when dry and green after watering. They should feel firm.
