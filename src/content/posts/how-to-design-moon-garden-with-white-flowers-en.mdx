## Quick Answer

Design a moon garden by combining white flowers, pale variegated foliage, silver leaves, fragrance, and soft path lighting where you spend evenings. Choose plants that reflect low light, such as white cosmos, nicotiana, moonflower vine, white phlox, jasmine, lamb's ear, dusty miller, and white hydrangeas.

## What makes a moon garden work at night?

A moon garden works because pale colours reflect low light better than saturated colours. Deep red, purple, and blue flowers can disappear after dusk, while white petals and silver foliage remain visible.

Texture matters as much as flower colour. Fine grasses, fuzzy silver leaves, glossy foliage, and rounded white flower heads catch light in different ways. The best moon gardens are layered, not just planted with white flowers in a row.

## Which white flowers are best for evening gardens?

Good choices include white cosmos, white zinnias, nicotiana, moonflower vine, white phlox, white impatiens for shade, white roses, white hydrangeas, and jasmine where climate allows.

For fragrance, prioritize nicotiana, jasmine, gardenia, tuberose, and evening-scented stock. Fragrance is strongest near seating, doors, and paths where people actually pass through at night.

## How should you place plants in a moon garden?

Put the brightest plants where they can be seen from windows, patios, and paths. A moon garden should be designed from the viewpoint where you spend evenings, not only from the daytime garden plan.

Use repetition. Three groups of white flowers along a path look calmer and more deliberate than ten unrelated white plants scattered everywhere. Add silver foliage between blooms so the effect remains after flowering peaks.

## What lighting should a moon garden use?

Use low, warm, indirect lighting. The goal is to support the planting, not flood it. Path lights, shielded uplights, and small warm fixtures near steps are usually enough.

Avoid harsh cool-white lights. They can make white flowers look flat and can overpower the reflective quality that gives a moon garden its character.

## What questions do gardeners ask about moon gardens?

### Do moon gardens need full sun?

Not always. Many white flowers need sun, but shade moon gardens can use white impatiens, hostas, caladiums, and variegated foliage.

### Are moonflowers the same as a moon garden?

No. Moonflower is one plant. A moon garden is a design style built around evening visibility and fragrance.

### What foliage works in a moon garden?

Silver foliage such as lamb's ear, artemisia, dusty miller, and lavender works well.

### Can a moon garden grow in containers?

Yes. Use white annuals, silver foliage, and fragrant plants in grouped pots near seating.
