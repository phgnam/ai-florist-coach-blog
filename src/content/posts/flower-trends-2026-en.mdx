The world of floristry is always evolving. In 2026, several exciting trends are shaping how florists
design, source, and present flowers. Here's what's gaining momentum.

## 1. Dried & dried-fresh hybrid arrangements

Combining **dried flowers** (pampas grass, dried roses, preserved leaves) with fresh blooms creates
arrangements that last weeks instead of days. This trend is both sustainable and visually striking.

## 2. Earthy, muted palettes

The era of vivid neon arrangements is giving way to **soft terracottas, dusty mauves, warm creams,
and sage greens**. These tones feel organic, romantic, and timeless.

## 3. Wild & unstructured designs

The carefully symmetrical bouquet is being replaced by loose, garden-picked arrangements that look
as if they were gathered from a meadow. **Asymmetry is intentional** — and beautiful.

## 4. Locally sourced and seasonal

Customers increasingly ask where their flowers come from. **Local, seasonal blooms** are in high
demand, both for their freshness and reduced environmental footprint.

## 5. AI-assisted design consultations

AI tools like **AI Florist Coach** are helping florists match flowers to occasions, colour palettes,
and client personalities faster than ever — freeing up creative time for the actual arranging.
