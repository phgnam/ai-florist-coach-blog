## What is the quick answer?

**Quick Answer:** Drought-tolerant cottage garden flowers include lavender, catmint, yarrow, salvias, gaura, verbena, echinacea, rudbeckia, sea holly, cosmos, and California poppy. The key is deep watering while plants establish, then mulch and lean soil. Avoid overfeeding, which makes soft thirsty growth.

## Which flowers give a cottage look without heavy watering?

Choose airy, repeat-flowering plants with relaxed shapes. Catmint, lavender, gaura, verbena, yarrow, salvias, cosmos, and echinacea all fit the cottage style while coping with drier conditions once established.

For structure, add rosemary, santolina, or small ornamental grasses. They keep the garden from looking empty when flowers pause.

## How should you prepare soil for drought-tolerant flowers?

Improve drainage before planting. Many drought-tolerant flowers dislike wet winter roots more than dry summer soil. Add compost where soil is poor, but do not make the bed overly rich.

Mulch after planting to reduce evaporation. Gravel mulch suits Mediterranean-style plants, while composted bark works for mixed borders.

## How should you water a waterwise cottage border?

Water deeply but less often. New plants need regular water for their first season. After that, encourage deeper roots by soaking the soil rather than sprinkling the surface.

Do not mistake drought-tolerant for never-water. In long heatwaves, even resilient plants may need support.

## What questions do gardeners ask about drought-tolerant cottage flowers?

### Can roses be drought-tolerant?

Established shrub roses can be fairly resilient, but they usually need more water than lavender, yarrow, or salvias.

### Are cosmos drought-tolerant?

Cosmos tolerate dry spells once established, but container cosmos need more regular watering.

### Should drought-tolerant flowers be fertilised?

Lightly, if at all. Too much fertiliser creates soft growth that needs more water.

### What mulch is best for lavender?

Gravel or grit mulch is often better than damp organic mulch around lavender crowns.
