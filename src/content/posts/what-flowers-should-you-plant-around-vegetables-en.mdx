## Quick Answer

Plant calendula, nasturtiums, marigolds, alyssum, borage, zinnias, cosmos, dill, cilantro, and sunflowers around vegetables. These flowers attract pollinators and beneficial insects, add edible blooms in some cases, and make vegetable beds easier to monitor. Keep tall flowers where they will not shade crops.

## Which flowers help bring pollinators to vegetables?

Borage, zinnias, cosmos, calendula, sunflowers, and alyssum are excellent for attracting bees, hoverflies, and other pollinators. They are especially useful near squash, cucumbers, melons, peppers, beans, and fruiting crops that depend on good pollination.

Plant flowers in small clusters rather than single scattered plants. Pollinators notice blocks of colour and return more often when food sources are easy to find.

## Which flowers help with pest balance?

Sweet alyssum attracts hoverflies, whose larvae feed on aphids. Calendula and marigolds bring in a wider range of small beneficial insects. Nasturtiums can act as a trap crop for aphids in some gardens, making infestations easier to spot and remove.

Flowers do not create a pest-proof garden. They improve the balance by making the bed more attractive to insects that hunt, parasitize, or compete with pests.

## Which edible flowers belong near vegetables?

Nasturtium, calendula, borage, chive blossoms, and violas are useful edible flowers. Keep them away from pesticide sprays and harvest only flowers you can identify confidently.

Edible flowers should still be treated as food crops. Grow them in clean soil, wash gently, and introduce them in small amounts if you have not eaten them before.

## How should you arrange flowers around vegetable beds?

Put low flowers along edges, medium flowers at row ends, and tall flowers on the north side of beds in the northern hemisphere so they do not shade vegetables. In very hot climates, strategic afternoon shade from taller flowers can help lettuce or herbs last longer.

Leave paths clear. A flower that spills into a walkway may look charming for a week, then become a slug shelter and watering obstacle.

## What questions do vegetable gardeners ask about flowers?

### Are marigolds really good for vegetable gardens?

Yes, but they are not magic. They attract beneficial insects and some types may help with soil pests when used correctly.

### Can flowers compete with vegetables?

Yes. Large flowers can compete for water, nutrients, and light if planted too close.

### Should herbs be allowed to flower near vegetables?

Often yes. Flowering dill, cilantro, basil, and chives attract many beneficial insects.

### What flower is easiest to start from seed near vegetables?

Calendula, nasturtium, cosmos, zinnia, and borage are all easy direct-sown choices.
