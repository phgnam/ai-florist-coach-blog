## Quick Answer

Hydrangea leaves usually turn brown at the edges because the plant is losing water faster than the roots can replace it. Common causes are hot afternoon sun, dry soil, shallow watering, root competition, wind, or excess fertiliser. Move potted plants into bright shade and water deeply before adding anything else.

## How can you tell what caused brown hydrangea leaf edges?

Look at the pattern. Dry, crisp brown edges on the sunniest side of the plant usually mean leaf scorch. Brown edges plus wilting by afternoon usually means water stress. Brown patches that appear soon after feeding can be fertiliser burn, especially if the soil was dry when fertiliser was applied.

Hydrangeas have broad leaves that lose moisture quickly. A plant can be watered often and still be dry at the root zone if each watering is shallow. Dig a small test hole beside the plant after watering. If only the top layer is damp, the root ball is still thirsty.

## What should you do first when hydrangea leaves brown?

Start with water and shade. Soak the root zone slowly until the soil is damp 20–30 cm down. Add mulch around the plant, keeping it a few centimetres away from the stems. If the plant is in a pot, move it away from hot walls, reflected paving heat, and afternoon sun.

Do not prune hard just because leaves look damaged. Brown leaf edges will not turn green again, but the damaged leaves still feed the plant if part of the leaf is healthy.

## When is brown edging a disease problem?

Disease usually makes spots, blotches, or fuzzy growth rather than a clean brown rim. Fungal leaf spots often begin as round or irregular marks scattered across the leaf. Scorch usually starts at the edges and tips, especially on exposed leaves.

Remove fallen diseased leaves and improve airflow if spots are spreading. Avoid overhead watering late in the day. For simple brown leaf edges, however, fungicide is rarely the answer.

## How can you prevent brown hydrangea leaves next season?

Plant hydrangeas where they receive morning sun and afternoon shade. Keep soil evenly moist, especially during bud formation and heatwaves. Add compost to improve water retention, then mulch after the soil warms.

For potted hydrangeas, use a large container with drainage holes. Small decorative pots heat up quickly and dry out too fast for mature plants.

## What questions do gardeners ask about brown hydrangea leaves?

### Should you cut off brown hydrangea leaves?

Remove fully brown leaves, but leave partly green leaves unless they are diseased. Green tissue still supports the plant.

### Can too much sun brown hydrangea leaves?

Yes. Hot afternoon sun is one of the most common causes of dry brown edges and scorched patches.

### Can overwatering cause brown hydrangea leaves?

Yes, but it usually comes with yellowing, soft growth, and poor drainage. Crisp edges more often point to drought or heat stress.

### Will brown hydrangea leaves recover?

The damaged tissue will not turn green again. New leaves can grow normally once the stress is corrected.
