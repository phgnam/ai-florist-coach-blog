## Quick Answer

Make a small cut flower patch by choosing a sunny bed, improving drainage, planting high-output annuals, and arranging them in rows for easy cutting. Start with zinnias, cosmos, snapdragons, calendula, sunflowers, and basil or mint for foliage. Sow small batches every few weeks for a longer harvest.

## How much space do you need for a cut flower patch?

A useful cut flower patch can be as small as 1 x 2 metres. The key is growing flowers for production rather than display. Rows, grids, and close spacing are practical because you will harvest stems before the planting looks like a traditional border.

Leave enough access to cut without stepping on soil. A narrow bed you can reach from both sides is easier to manage than a wide square bed.

## Which flowers give the most stems in a small patch?

Choose cut-and-come-again annuals. Zinnias, cosmos, calendula, snapdragons, scabiosa, celosia, and branching sunflowers produce repeatedly when cut. Add basil, mint, scented geranium, or eucalyptus where climate allows for foliage.

Avoid filling the whole patch with one-time flowers unless you succession sow them. Single-stem sunflowers are beautiful, but each plant gives one main cut.

## How should you prepare the bed?

Remove perennial weeds, loosen compacted soil, and add compost. Cut flowers need steady growth, but they do not all want heavy feeding. Good soil structure and consistent moisture matter more than excessive fertiliser.

Install support before plants need it. Netting, low stakes, or simple twine grids stop stems from leaning after rain. It is much harder to support a crowded patch once stems are already bent.

## How do you keep flowers coming all season?

Succession sow. Plant a small batch of fast annuals, then sow another batch two or three weeks later. This prevents the whole patch from peaking at once.

Cut regularly, even if you do not need a bouquet. Leaving mature flowers on the plant tells many annuals to slow down and set seed.

## What questions do new cut-flower growers ask?

### Does a cut flower patch need full sun?

Most cut flowers need at least six hours of sun. Some, such as foxgloves and sweet peas, tolerate cooler partial sun.

### Can you grow cut flowers in a raised bed?

Yes. Raised beds are excellent because drainage, spacing, and access are easy to control.

### How often should you cut flowers?

Cut every two or three days during peak bloom to keep plants productive.

### What is the easiest cut flower from seed?

Zinnias, cosmos, calendula, and sunflowers are among the easiest beginner cut flowers.
