## What is the quick answer?

**Quick Answer:** Make edible flower ice cubes by using pesticide-free edible blooms, rinsing them gently, placing one or two petals in each tray cell, filling halfway with boiled cooled water, freezing, then topping up and freezing again. Use violas, borage, calendula petals, rose petals, nasturtium, or herb flowers.

## Which flowers are safe for ice cubes?

Use only flowers you can identify as edible and that have not been sprayed. Good choices include violas, pansies, borage, calendula petals, nasturtium, rose petals, lavender, chamomile, and chive blossoms.

Avoid florist flowers unless they are sold specifically as edible. Many cut flowers are grown for display, not food use.

## How do you keep flowers centered in the ice?

Freeze in two layers. Fill the tray halfway, add petals, and freeze. Then top up with more water and freeze again. This keeps petals from floating only at the top.

For clearer ice, use boiled and cooled water or filtered water. Perfectly clear ice is hard in a normal freezer, but the layered method still looks polished.

## What drinks work best with flower ice cubes?

Use flower ice in lemonade, iced tea, sparkling water, garden cocktails, and fruit punches. Match strong flavours carefully. Lavender can dominate delicate drinks, while viola and borage are much milder.

Serve the cubes soon after removing them from the freezer. Thin petals look best before the ice melts too far.

## What questions do hosts ask about flower ice cubes?

### Can you use rose petals in ice cubes?

Yes, if the roses are pesticide-free and grown for edible use.

### Why did my flowers float to the top?

They float because they are lighter than water. Freeze the cubes in two layers to hold them in place.

### Can you make flower ice cubes ahead?

Yes. Make them a few days ahead and store in a sealed freezer bag to prevent freezer odours.

### Are all pansies edible?

Pansies are generally edible, but only use plants grown without unsafe sprays.
