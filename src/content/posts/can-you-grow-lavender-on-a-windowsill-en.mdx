## Quick Answer

You can grow lavender on a windowsill only if it receives very bright direct light, excellent drainage, and careful watering. A south or west-facing window is best. Use a gritty potting mix, let the top half of the pot dry before watering, and choose compact English lavender varieties.

## Which windowsill is bright enough for lavender?

Lavender needs stronger light than most indoor herbs. A south-facing windowsill is the best indoor position in the northern hemisphere, with a west-facing window as the next choice. East windows may work in summer but often fail in winter.

If the plant stretches, leans, or makes soft pale growth, it is not getting enough light. Rotate the pot weekly, but do not expect rotation to fix a dark room. Lavender is a sun plant first and an indoor plant only under the right conditions.

## What pot and soil does windowsill lavender need?

Use a terracotta pot with drainage holes. Terracotta dries faster than plastic, which suits lavender's preference for air around the roots. The pot should be only slightly larger than the root ball; oversized pots stay wet for too long.

Mix standard potting compost with perlite, pumice, or coarse sand. The finished mix should feel loose and gritty, not heavy and damp.

## How often should you water indoor lavender?

Water when the top half of the pot has dried. Push a finger or wooden skewer into the mix to check moisture below the surface. If the lower pot still feels damp, wait.

When you do water, soak thoroughly and let excess water drain away. Never leave lavender sitting in a saucer of water. Chronic damp soil causes root rot long before the leaves look obviously sick.

## How do you prune lavender on a windowsill?

Trim lightly after flowering or when stems become leggy. Cut back into green leafy growth, not old bare wood. Lavender does not reliably resprout from hard woody stems indoors.

Pinch young tips to keep the plant compact. Indoor lavender often grows less densely than outdoor lavender, so small regular trims are better than one severe cut.

## What questions do growers ask about indoor lavender?

### Will lavender flower indoors?

It can flower indoors if it gets strong direct light, but many windowsill plants bloom less heavily than outdoor plants.

### Why is indoor lavender turning grey and crispy?

It may be too dry, too hot against glass, or suffering root damage from previous overwatering.

### Why is indoor lavender wilting after watering?

Wilting after watering often means the roots are stressed by soggy soil or poor drainage.

### Can lavender grow under a grow light?

Yes. A strong full-spectrum grow light for 12–14 hours daily can keep lavender healthier than a dim window.
