Roses are among the most beloved flowers in the world — but they're also famously **delicate**.
Here are the secrets to keeping your roses fresh and vibrant far longer than usual.

## 1. Cut the stems correctly

When you bring roses home, cut the base of each stem at a **45-degree angle** under running water.
This increases the surface area for water uptake and prevents air from blocking the stem.

## 2. Change the water regularly

Replace the water in your vase **every 1–2 days**. Fresh water prevents bacteria from building up
and accelerating wilting.

## 3. Choose the right spot

- Keep roses away from **direct sunlight through windows**
- Away from **air conditioning vents or fans**
- Never place them near **ripe fruit** — the ethylene gas speeds up wilting

## 4. Add nutrients

Add a small amount of **sugar** (1 teaspoon per litre of water) or use a commercial flower
preservative sachet to nourish the flowers.

## 5. The ideal temperature

Roses stay freshest at **15–18 °C**. If possible, move them to the refrigerator overnight.

---

Apply these tips and your roses can stay vibrant for an extra **5–7 days** compared to usual!
