## What is the quick answer?

**Quick Answer:** Plant a bulb lasagna pot by layering large late bulbs deepest, medium bulbs in the middle, and small early bulbs near the top. Use a deep container with drainage, free-draining potting mix, and firm spacing. Water once after planting, then keep the pot cool and lightly moist through winter.

## Which bulbs go in each layer?

Put the largest and latest bulbs at the bottom: tulips or large daffodils. Add a layer of potting mix, then place medium bulbs such as hyacinths or smaller narcissus. Near the top, add small early bulbs such as crocus, grape hyacinth, iris reticulata, or snowdrops.

The idea is simple: small early bulbs flower first, then middle bulbs, then the deeper larger bulbs.

## How deep should the container be?

Use a pot at least 30 cm deep. Deeper is better if you want three full bulb layers. The pot must have drainage holes because bulbs rot in waterlogged compost.

You can plant bulbs closer in pots than in the ground, but they should not be pressed hard against each other. Leave a small gap so roots can grow and water can move.

## How do you care for the pot after planting?

Water the pot after planting to settle the compost. Then keep it cool outdoors or in an unheated space. The mix should be lightly moist, not wet. If winter is very rainy, move the pot where it will not stay saturated.

When shoots appear, move the pot into its display position. Rotate it occasionally so stems do not lean strongly toward the light.

## What questions do gardeners ask about bulb lasagna pots?

### Can tulips and daffodils go in the same pot?

Yes. Plant large daffodils and tulips in the deeper layers and smaller bulbs above them.

### Should bulb tips point upward?

Yes. Plant pointed ends up for tulips, daffodils, hyacinths, and most common bulbs.

### Can you reuse bulbs after flowering?

Daffodils often return better than tulips. Feed after flowering and let leaves die back naturally.

### When should you plant a bulb lasagna pot?

Plant in autumn, before the ground or potting mix freezes in cold climates.
