To make a flower crown, bend **floral wire** into a head-sized circle, wrap it in **floral tape**, then attach small flower bundles by wrapping their stems to the wire base with more tape. Fresh crowns last 4–8 hours; dried or preserved flower crowns last months. The whole process takes 30–60 minutes.

## What materials do you need for a flower crown?

Gather these before you start — nothing exotic, and most are available at craft stores:

- **Floral wire** (18–20 gauge) — the structural base. Flexible enough to bend into a circle, rigid enough to hold shape on your head
- **Floral tape** — self-sealing stretch tape that sticks to itself. It covers the wire, provides grip for attaching flowers, and hides mechanics
- **Wire cutters** or strong scissors
- **Flowers and greenery** — see variety recommendations below
- **Optional**: thin paddle wire (28 gauge) for securing stubborn stems, ribbon for trailing tails

**For fresh crowns**: Cut flowers 2–3 hours before assembly and keep them in water. Shorter stems (8–12 cm) work better than long ones.

**For dried crowns**: Dried flowers are lighter and easier to work with. Lavender, baby's breath, strawflowers, statice, and small dried roses are excellent choices.

## How do you make the wire base?

1. Cut a piece of floral wire about **60 cm** long (adjust for your head size — you want it to sit comfortably with a little overlap)
2. Form it into a **circle**, overlapping the ends by 3–4 cm
3. Twist the overlapping ends together tightly — this is the back of the crown
4. Try it on — it should sit on your head like a headband, not squeeze
5. Wrap the entire wire circle in **floral tape**, stretching the tape slightly as you go (stretching activates the adhesive). This creates a non-slip base that flowers attach to easily

For a more natural look, wrap the base in **two layers** of tape. The thicker surface grips flower stems better.

## How do you attach flowers to the crown?

This is the core technique — once you learn the wrapping motion, it goes quickly:

1. **Make mini bundles**: Group 2–3 stems together — typically one focal flower (rose, ranunculus) with filler (baby's breath, wax flower) and a small piece of greenery (eucalyptus, fern)
2. **Trim stems** to 5–7 cm
3. **Place the first bundle** against the wire base, stems pointing along the wire (not sticking out)
4. **Wrap floral tape** tightly around the stems and wire together — 3–4 tight wraps secures it
5. **Place the next bundle** overlapping the previous one — the flower heads of bundle two should cover the wrapped stems of bundle one
6. **Continue around the crown**, always overlapping in the same direction
7. **Finish** by tucking the last bundle's stems under the first bundle's flowers and taping securely

**Spacing tip**: Place bundles every 5–7 cm for a full, lush crown. For a more minimal, airy look, space them 8–10 cm apart and use more greenery.

## Which flowers work best for fresh crowns?

The best flower crown flowers are **small to medium sized**, **hardy** (they won't wilt in 20 minutes), and have **flexible stems**:

**Excellent choices**:
- Spray roses — small, sturdy, available in every colour
- Ranunculus — stunning layers of petals, holds well for hours
- Wax flower — delicate filler with excellent longevity
- Baby's breath — classic filler, lasts all day
- Eucalyptus — aromatic, structural greenery that barely wilts
- Astilbe — feathery texture, beautiful in pastels
- Statice — dries beautifully, so the crown becomes a keepsake

**Avoid**: Large-headed flowers (full dahlias, garden roses with big heads) — they're too heavy. Delicate flowers that wilt fast (sweet peas, poppies, gardenias) — they'll look sad within an hour.

## How do you make a dried flower crown that lasts?

Dried flower crowns are actually easier to make and last for months:

1. Use the same wire base technique
2. Choose sturdy dried flowers: **strawflowers, dried baby's breath, lavender, statice, small dried roses, bunny tails, amaranth**
3. Attach with floral tape — dried stems are more brittle, so wrap gently but firmly
4. **Hot glue** is an alternative to tape for dried crowns — apply a small dot where the stem meets the wire and press for 5 seconds
5. Spray the finished crown lightly with **unscented hairspray** to reduce shedding

Dried crowns make excellent wall decor after wearing. Hang on a small hook and they'll last 6–12 months before colours fade significantly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does a fresh flower crown last?**
4–8 hours in moderate weather. Keep it in a plastic bag in the fridge until wearing, mist with water, and avoid direct sun. In hot weather (30°C+), some flowers may wilt within 2–3 hours.

**Can you make a flower crown the night before?**
Yes — assemble it, mist lightly with water, wrap loosely in damp paper towels, place in a plastic bag (not sealed), and refrigerate. It should last 12–18 hours this way.

**How much does it cost to make a flower crown?**
Materials (wire, tape) cost about $5–10. Fresh flowers from a grocery store or garden add $10–25. Total: $15–35, compared to $60–150 from a florist.
